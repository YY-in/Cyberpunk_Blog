## Overview

Developed a music rhythm perception VR game prototype as part of academic research at the University of Nottingham. The project implements real-time audio analysis to visualize the correlation between music rhythm and human body movement in virtual space, while recording interaction data to support research.

## Research Methodology

- Conducted multi-dimensional user research including observation, questionnaires, and interviews
- Preliminarily revealed six paradigms of human rhythmic perception and computer interaction
- Provided foundational support for subsequent HCI research directions

## Technical Specs

| Component | Technology |
|-----------|------------|
| Platform | VR (Unity) |
| Audio | Real-time frequency analysis |
| Data | Interaction logging + visualization |
| Research | Mixed methods (Observation, Survey, Interview) |

## Key Findings

- Identified 6 paradigms linking human rhythmic perception to computational interaction
- Real-time audio-visual feedback significantly enhances rhythmic awareness in VR
- Interaction data collection framework applicable to broader HCI rhythm studies
